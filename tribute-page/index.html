Shaykh al-Islam Ibn Taymiyyah

About

Shaykh al-Islām Ibn Taymiyyah, his nam: Taqī ad-Dīn 'Aḥmad ibn Abd al-Ḥalīm ibn ʿAbd al-Salām al-Numayrī al-Ḥarrānī (in Arabic "تقي الدين أحمد بن عبدالحليم بن عبدالسلام النميري الحراني") Ibn Taymiyyah's own relationship, as a religious scholar, with the ruling apparatus was not always amicable. It ranged from silence to open rebellion. On occasions when he shared the same views and aims as the ruling authorities his contributions were welcomed, but when Ibn Taymiyyah went against the status quo, he was seen as "uncooperative", and on occasions spent much time in prison. Ibn Taymiyyah's attitude towards his own rulers was based on the actions of Muhammad's companions when they made an oath of allegiance to him as follows; "to obey within obedience to God, even if the one giving the order is unjust; to abstain from disputing the authority of those who exert it; and to speak out the truth, or take up its cause without fear in respect of God, of blame from anyone."

achievements

683 AH

He taught the Qur’an, interpretation and Iftaa when he was seventeen years old

698 AH

Writing "Fatwa alhamawia" to respond to the people of Hama in their question about the attributes of God contained in the Qur'an. This fatwa contradicted the method of Ash'ari and theological scholars, which led to his being summoned to the judicial council, but he did not respond.

698 AH

The Wasitiyya doctrine was written at the request of the rulers of the city of Wasit, after ignorance spread and the people became heresy, so they sent to the Sheikh to explain the doctrine of the Sunnis and the group

700 AH

He participated in fighting the Tatars, repelling them from the Levant, and inciting the believers to fight and not to hurry to escape. And as he incited the sultans and rulers to support the Muslims
